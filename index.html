About

I've always wanted to create something useful, meaningful, and visually appealing, which is why I started learning front-end development two years ago. Projects

Fitness Hub

Introducing Fitness Hub, a front-end project that seamlessly integrates React, Styled-Components, Redux, and TypeScript. This platform is designed with three core functionalities: Exercises, Food, and Calculators. Explore a diverse range of exercises, each accompanied by detailed instructions and visual demonstrations. search for any food item and accessits nutritional information instantly. calculate your daily calorie intake and BMI.This project served as a hands-on opportunity for me to sharpen my TypeScript skills, demonstrating my dedication to staying current with industry best practices

Spotify Clone

The Spotify Clone project closely mimics Spotify's design and essential features, with the exception of certain functionalities not accessible through the Spotify API. It was my most ambitious project completed independently, without the guidance of online courses, and it took me one month to build. During this project, I encountered and overcame several challenges. One of the main difficulties was implementing a music player that allowed users to play, pause, rewind, and skip tracks. Additionally, managing the application's state, handling routing, and dealing with dynamic data were complex tasks. Nevertheless, these challenges provided valuable learning experiences and contributed to my growth as a developer. For this project, I used React for building the user interface and Styled-Components for styling. To manage the global state of the application effectively, I utilized React's useContext and useReducer hooks.

Comfy Sloth

The Comfy Sloth is a website that encompasses nearly every feature of a classic e-commerce website. It includes a landing page, products page, authentication, a shopping cart, and a payment gateway. This project provided valuable lessons, as I learned how an e-commerce website operates. The most challenging aspects were implementing the products page with its filtering and sorting functionalities, as well as the shopping cart. However, I managed them successfully by utilizing React and Styled Components for this project. Additionally, I employed useContext and useReducer for managing the global state.
